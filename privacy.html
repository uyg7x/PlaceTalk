Privacy Policy

PlaceTalk is built to help people discover nearby places with a light, privacy-first experience. This page explains what information may be used when people browse the website, allow location access, or submit community content.

Last updated 20 March 2026 Quick summary The site can use browser location only after permission is granted. Nearby results may also depend on public mapping sources and external links selected by the user.

1. Information we may use

  • Location data: your browser location is used only to show nearby places after you allow access.
  • Search and preference data: category choices, saved places, and simple UI preferences may be stored in your browser using local storage.
  • Community submissions: if you add a place, review, photo, or note, that information can be displayed inside the website experience.
  • Technical information: hosting and browser systems may process standard request information such as IP address, browser type, or basic analytics needed to deliver the site.

2. How this information is used

PlaceTalk uses the information above to show nearby results, improve category feeds, remember simple user choices on the same device, support community-added places, and keep the service running properly.

3. Third-party services and links

The website may connect users to external services such as OpenStreetMap-based data sources, map or directions links, restaurant platform search links, and social pages. Those services operate under their own policies, terms, and data practices.

4. Storage and retention

Some preferences may remain in the browser until cleared by the user. Community content may remain visible until removed or updated. Exact retention can depend on future product changes and the hosting environment used for the site.

5. Your choices

  • You can deny or revoke browser location access at any time.
  • You can clear local browser storage to remove saved preferences on your device.
  • You can request correction or removal of community content by using the contact details on the contact page.

6. Children and sensitive data

PlaceTalk is intended for general audiences and is not designed to intentionally collect sensitive personal information or special categories of personal data. Users should avoid submitting private or sensitive information through public community fields.

7. Contact

For privacy questions, removal requests, or corrections, visit the Contact page.
